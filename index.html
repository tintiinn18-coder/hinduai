What does the Gita say about dealing with fear?
Chapter 2, Verse 14 — Bhagavad Gita "O son of Kunti, the non-permanent appearance of happiness and distress, and their disappearance in due course, are like the appearance and disappearance of winter and summer seasons."

Krishna teaches that fear arises when we are attached to outcomes. When you act from duty (Dharma) without craving results, fear dissolves. You are the eternal Atman — nothing can truly harm you. 🪔
Tell me a story from Ramayana about patience
🏹 Sita's Patience in Ashoka Vatika

When Sita was held in Lanka, she never lost faith. Even in Ravana's golden palace, she chose the ground, holding a blade of grass between herself and her captor. Her patience and devotion to Rama was her greatest strength.

Lesson: True patience is not weakness — it is unshakeable faith in Dharma. 🌿
